Mirrors vs. Lenses

Mirrors reflect light. Lenses are transparent and refract (bend) light. The terms Convex (bulging out) and Concave (caving in) describe their geometric shapes, not their material. A lens is NOT made of mirrors.

Real Images

Formed when light rays physically cross in space. They are always inverted.

Real Life: Used whenever an image must be captured on a physical surface, such as a cinema screen, a digital camera sensor, or the retina of your eye.

Virtual Images

Formed when light rays diverge. Your brain traces them backward to an illusionary point of origin. They are always upright and cannot be projected.

Real Life: Used when you look into an optic to see a modified view, such as bathroom mirrors, magnifying glasses, or corrective eyeglasses.
